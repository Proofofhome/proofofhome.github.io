# Resources

Books that actually shaped how I think about this project. Not trying to give you the standard Bitcoin reading list — a couple of these are the obvious picks, but most aren't.

## Money

- **The Bitcoin Standard** — Saifedean Ammous. Still the right starting point for the monetary side, even if you've heard it cited a hundred times.
- **Mastering Bitcoin** — Andreas Antonopoulos. The technical companion to the above — actually explains what your node is doing, not just why it matters.

## Systems & Sovereignty

- **Seeing Like a State** — James C. Scott. Not a Bitcoin book at all, but it's the best explanation I've read for why centralized systems flatten and simplify things to make them controllable — which is a big part of why running your own infrastructure matters.
- **Antifragile** — Nassim Nicholas Taleb. The idea that systems get stronger from stress and failure, not despite it. Basically the whole reason I publish the mistakes.

## Self-Reliance

- **Walden** — Henry David Thoreau. Old, a bit slow, still worth it.
- **Small Is Beautiful** — E.F. Schumacher. Economics at human scale, written decades before "self-hosting" was a phrase anyone used.
- **The Self-Sufficient Life and How to Live It** — John Seymour. Practical homesteading reference — ties into where the resilience/prepping season is headed.

## Technology & Open Source

- **The Cathedral and the Bazaar** — Eric Raymond. The original case for open development over closed, black-box systems.
- **In the Beginning… Was the Command Line** — Neal Stephenson. Short, funny, and about choosing tools you can actually see inside rather than ones you just have to trust.
