Why I Started ProofOfHome: Taking Back Control in a More Controlled World
I’m a regular guy — not a tech wizard, not a prepper, just an average Australian who a while back stumbled into Bitcoin almost by accident. I started reading about money, how it works, and the idea of self-reliance. Something just clicked. Bitcoin wasn’t just “magic internet money” to me — it felt like a tool for taking back control in a world that increasingly wants to manage everything for you.
The more I read, the more I saw the bigger picture: governments are getting more socialist by the day — taking a bigger cut of your money through taxes and policies, while slowly chipping away at personal freedoms under the banner of safety and fairness.
Australia shows the trend clearly:

COVID-era lockdowns, tracing apps, credit card tracking, and even home surveillance ideas set dangerous precedents for control.
Now we have digital ID pushes, facial recognition databases, social media age bans with talk of cracking down on VPNs, and rules that push platforms to track users more aggressively.
Capital gains tax changes that hand a big slice of investment gains to the government, growing interest in superannuation pools, and broader surveillance powers.

It feels like more of your money, data, and daily choices are being directed from the top, leaving normal households more dependent than ever.
That realisation hit me hard. So I started ProofOfHome — an open-source project to help regular people like me build real practical independence at home.
You don’t need to be an expert. I’m definitely not. If a normal person like me can start figuring this stuff out after simply reading about Bitcoin, so can you. The focus is on simple, doable steps for everyday resilience:

Running your own Bitcoin node — learn to verify your money instead of blindly trusting banks or apps, especially as taxes and financial rules tighten.
Self-hosting basic services — your files, photos, or even a personal email/calendar so you’re not fully at the mercy of big platforms.
Using open-source tools — software you can actually understand and trust more.
Adding solar or basic energy backup — small steps toward not being completely reliant on the grid.
Repairing old tech instead of constantly buying new — saves money and reduces dependency.

The core idea is straightforward: the world is getting more digital and more regulated. Normal people who learn to control their own little corner — money, data, power, and tools — will have more options and peace of mind.
ProofOfHome isn’t about disappearing off-grid. It’s about becoming more capable right where you are. One small win at a time.
Tagline: "Building sovereign technology one home at a time."
If you’re an average person feeling the same squeeze — higher costs, less privacy, more rules — start small with me. Run a node, self-host something simple, fix what’s broken instead of replacing it. The project is completely open. Share what you’re trying. What’s one thing you’re curious to learn first?
